Let the art speak
Minimal web design
When I was approached by Christina George, a fantastic painter and my lovely wife, to build a portfolio site to support the sales of her work, I knew it had to be simple and elegant. We worked together to establish a clear page structure using pen-and-paper prototyping, and then I iterated through a number of revisions until we were satisfied with the single page site.

While researching artist portfolios in preparation for this project, I noticed a common trend. Whether featured on Behance, awwwards.com, or CSS Design Awards, the typical acclaimed artist website draws more attention to itself than to the artist it represents. How is this good design?
